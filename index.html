About

Seventeen

Seventeen is a South Korean boy band formed by Pledis Entertainment. They debuted on May 26, 2015, with their extended play (EP) titled "17 Carat." The group consists of thirteen members, who are divided into three sub-units: the hip-hop unit, the vocal unit, and the performance unit. Each unit has its own unique style and showcases different talents.

Members of Seventeen:
1. S.Coups (Leader, Rapper)
2. Jeonghan (Vocalist)
3. Joshua (Vocalist)
4. Jun (Vocalist)
5. Hoshi (Leader of Performance Unit, Main Dancer, Rapper)
6. Wonwoo (Rapper)
7. Woozi (Leader of Vocal Unit, Main Vocalist)
8. DK (Main Vocalist)
9. Mingyu (Rapper)
10. The8 (Dancer, Rapper)
11. Seungkwan (Main Vocalist)
12. Vernon (Rapper)
13. Dino (Main Dancer, Rapper, Maknae/Youngest Member)

Seventeen is known for their self-produced music and choreography, as many of the members actively participate in the songwriting, producing, and arranging of their songs. They have released several successful albums and EPs, including "Love & Letter," "Teen, Age," "An Ode," and "Your Choice."
The group's music style encompasses various genres, including pop, hip-hop, R&B, and EDM. They are recognized for their energetic performances, intricate choreography, and synchronized teamwork. Seventeen has gained a significant following both domestically in South Korea and internationally, with a dedicated fanbase known as "Carats."
Seventeen has received numerous awards and accolades throughout their career, including multiple wins on music shows, Daesang (Grand Prize) awards, and international recognition. They have embarked on several world tours, including "Diamond Edge," "Ideal Cut," and "Ode to You," showcasing their talent and stage presence to audiences around the world.
In addition to their music activities, Seventeen members have also pursued individual endeavors, such as appearing on television shows, hosting radio programs, and participating in acting projects.
